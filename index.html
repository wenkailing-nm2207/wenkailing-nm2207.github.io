Why Does This Matter?

Crime and murder exist all over the world, though its prevalence varies across different countries. Why do these differences exist? In this data story, I will attempt to use the Human Development Index (HDI) as a tool to answer the question:
How are murder rates influenced by the human development index?

How does the Human Development Index relate to crime rate?

First, let's break down the components of HDI:

Health Level

Measure via life expectancy.

Educational Level

Measured via mean years of schooling completed and expected years of schooling upon entering the education system.

Living Standard

Measured via per capita income.

HDI increased from 2005 to 2015 for both countries, with Singapore consistently having a significantly higher HDI than Moldova.

Murder rates were determined by observing the number of murders per 100k people. Murder rates decreased from 2005 to 2015, with Singapore consistently having a significantly lower murder rate than Moldova.

How can we further reduce murder rates?

An important factor of HDI in predicting murder rates is education. The more educated people are, the more they value life and engage in less criminal activities. As such, it is crucial to provide accessible education to all citizens in order to bring down the number of homicide cases.
